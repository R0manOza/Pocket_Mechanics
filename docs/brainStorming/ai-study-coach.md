## H1: Hassle

*What process in your daily life is tedious, error-prone, or needlessly manual?*

**WHO has this problem?**  
Students preparing for midterms/finals who have lecture slides, PDFs, and random notes, but don’t know what to study first or whether they actually understand the material.

**PROBLEM in one sentence:**  
They spend hours “studying” (re-reading/highlighting) but still perform poorly because their study method is passive and they don’t get feedback on what they truly know.

**CURRENT SOLUTION:**  
They re-read slides, watch videos, copy notes, or cram the night before; some make flashcards manually, but it takes too long and they still don’t prioritize the right topics.

**AI ANGLE:**  
Personalized active-recall coach: the user uploads slides/notes, the AI generates practice questions (varying difficulty), schedules spaced repetition, and adapts based on the user’s answers (weak areas get more practice).

**WHY IT MATTERS:**  
Better learning with less time: students can turn messy materials into targeted practice, reduce anxiety, and avoid last-minute cramming because they see progress and gaps early.

## H2: Hardship

*What problem do you see in your community or country that affects real people?*

**WHO has this problem?**  
Students who don’t have access to tutors or strong academic support networks (especially first-year students or students in difficult gateway courses).

**PROBLEM in one sentence:**  
They get stuck on concepts and fall behind because they don’t receive timely explanation + practice that matches their exact confusion level.

**CURRENT SOLUTION:**  
They search YouTube, ask friends, or post in group chats; answers are inconsistent, and students may feel embarrassed to ask the same thing multiple times.

**AI ANGLE:**  
“Explain-then-drill” tutoring: the AI gives a short explanation and immediately follows with practice questions; it detects misconceptions from the student’s answers and re-explains in a different way.

**WHY IT MATTERS:**  
More equal outcomes: a low-cost study coach can provide consistent practice and explanations, helping students pass difficult courses and reducing dropout risk.

## H3: Horizon

*What new capability does multimodal AI unlock that was impossible 2 years ago?*

**WHO has this problem?**  
Students who study from screenshots/photos of whiteboards, handwritten notes, or mixed-format materials (PDFs + images + tables).

**PROBLEM in one sentence:**  
Their best study content isn’t clean text, so it can’t be easily turned into flashcards, quizzes, or a structured study plan.

**CURRENT SOLUTION:**  
Manual rewriting into text, or ignoring those materials; they lose valuable examples and instructor-specific hints that often appear only on a board or in annotations.

**AI ANGLE:**  
Multimodal extraction + understanding: convert screenshots/handwritten notes into structured concepts, then generate quizzes and a study plan (topics → subtopics → practice sets).

**WHY IT MATTERS:**  
Students can transform real class materials into high-quality practice quickly, which is especially helpful when exams are based on instructor-specific examples.
