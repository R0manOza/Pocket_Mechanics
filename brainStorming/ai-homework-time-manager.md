## H1: Hassle

*What process in your daily life is tedious, error-prone, or needlessly manual?*

**WHO has this problem?**  
University students who have 4–6 classes at the same time and keep track of deadlines across multiple platforms (LMS, email, chat groups), especially students who also work part-time.

**PROBLEM in one sentence:**  
They miss deadlines or start assignments too late because their tasks and time are fragmented across apps and they don’t have a clear, realistic plan for the week.

**CURRENT SOLUTION:**  
Manual to-do lists, calendar reminders, sticky notes, or “I’ll remember it”; students often copy deadlines from the LMS by hand and underestimate how long work will take.

**AI ANGLE:**  
Natural-language task extraction + scheduling optimization: the user pastes an assignment description (or uploads a screenshot), the AI extracts deliverables + due dates + subtasks, estimates effort, and generates a day-by-day plan that adapts when something changes.

**WHY IT MATTERS:**  
Fewer missed deadlines, less last-minute stress, better grades, and better sleep—because the plan is realistic and updates automatically when the week gets chaotic.

## H2: Hardship

*What problem do you see in your community or country that affects real people?*

**WHO has this problem?**  
Students with limited academic support (first-generation students, students balancing jobs/family responsibilities) who struggle with planning and prioritizing—not intelligence.

**PROBLEM in one sentence:**  
They fall behind not because the material is impossible, but because they can’t reliably organize tasks, break work into steps, and keep momentum under real-life constraints.

**CURRENT SOLUTION:**  
They try generic productivity apps, or they rely on friends; when they get behind, they do “all-nighters” and burnout cycles repeat.

**AI ANGLE:**  
Adaptive planning assistant that turns course requirements into a structured plan (subtasks + time blocks) and re-plans when the user loses time, with short check-ins and accountability prompts.

**WHY IT MATTERS:**  
Better retention and mental health: if planning becomes easier, students are less likely to drop classes or fail due to chaos rather than capability.

## H3: Horizon

*What new capability does multimodal AI unlock that was impossible 2 years ago?*

**WHO has this problem?**  
Students who receive tasks in messy formats (screenshots from group chats, photos of a whiteboard, PDFs, mixed-language instructions).

**PROBLEM in one sentence:**  
Important assignment details get lost because the “source of truth” is scattered and not machine-readable, so students waste time re-checking and still miss key requirements.

**CURRENT SOLUTION:**  
Manual copy-paste, rewriting tasks into notes, or asking someone else “what exactly is due?”; it’s slow and error-prone.

**AI ANGLE:**  
Multimodal extraction (screenshot/photo → structured tasks) + summarization + conversion into a schedule and checklist; the AI can also generate a “requirements checklist” for each assignment.

**WHY IT MATTERS:**  
Students spend less time organizing and more time doing the work; the risk of “I misunderstood the requirement” drops because the AI outputs a clear checklist.
